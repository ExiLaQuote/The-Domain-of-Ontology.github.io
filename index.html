We know that this picture of everything is not everything.
We're not in the picture, after all. But maybe you don't have to be, because maybe you don't really exist!
What about the "things" that cannot be seen, that which can never be painted or drawn?

How then must we identify everything there is? Can such things be classified? And what are these things like?

  • Aristotle first described ontology when he called it "first philosophy" in Metaphysics Book IV.
  • It became ontologia or "science of being." The field would then be developed by Wolff, Husserl, and Quine.
